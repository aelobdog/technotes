Introduction

Bzip2 is a fascinating algorithm. It is very different when compared to other compresseion techniques I've come across. I've been pretty invested in the Lempel-Ziv land of compression algorithms (yes, I'm going to use "algorithm" and "technique" interchangeably throughout the article) which is probably why bzip2 feels like a breath of fresh air to me right now.

Now obviously, since the BWT is not a compression algorithm in the sense that it does not actually reduce the number of bytes requires to store data, it has to be used alongside other known algorithms like RLE, and Huffman compression. These techniques I am familiar with, because the beginning of my compression journey happened with these techniques. These techniques also appear in other techniques like gzip due to their simplicity and effectiveness.

Since I'm going to explore bzip2, its documentation and codebase, I might as well document what I learn. In the event that I forget what I have studied, this might come in handy. And who knows, maybe this article catches the eye of someone looking for something interesting and gets inspired by it?
